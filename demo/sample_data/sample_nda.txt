NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of
February 1, 2026, by and between:

  Acme Corp, a Delaware corporation with its principal office at
  1200 Industrial Blvd, Suite 400, Vancouver, BC V6B 1A1 ("Discloser")

  AND

  TechStart Inc., a British Columbia corporation with its principal
  office at 456 Innovation Drive, Vancouver, BC V5K 0A1 ("Recipient")

Collectively referred to as the "Parties."

------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

1.1  "Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information
     disclosed by Discloser to Recipient, whether orally, in writing, or
     by electronic means, including but not limited to: trade secrets,
     business plans, customer lists, financial data, product roadmaps,
     technical specifications, source code, algorithms, and marketing
     strategies.

1.2  Confidential Information does not include information that:
     (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of Recipient;
     (b) was rightfully in Recipient's possession prior to disclosure;
     (c) is independently developed by Recipient without use of or
         reference to the Confidential Information; or
     (d) is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. CONFIDENTIALITY OBLIGATIONS

2.1  Recipient agrees to hold all Confidential Information in strict
     confidence and to not disclose such information to any third party
     without the prior written consent of Discloser.

2.2  Recipient shall use the Confidential Information solely for the
     purpose of evaluating a potential business relationship between
     the Parties ("Permitted Purpose").

2.3  Recipient shall protect the Confidential Information using the
     same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential
     information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

2.4  The obligations of confidentiality shall remain in effect for a
     period of two (2) years from the date of disclosure of each item
     of Confidential Information.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. NON-COMPETE CLAUSE

3.1  During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12)
     months following its termination, Recipient agrees not to directly
     or indirectly engage in any business that competes with Discloser's
     primary line of business within a fifty (50) mile radius of
     Discloser's principal office in Vancouver, BC.

3.2  This non-compete restriction applies to Recipient's officers,
     directors, employees, and agents who have had access to the
     Confidential Information.

3.3  The Parties acknowledge that this non-compete provision is
     reasonable in scope, duration, and geographic area, and is
     necessary to protect Discloser's legitimate business interests.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

4.1  Any inventions, discoveries, improvements, or works of authorship
     conceived or developed by Recipient using or derived from the
     Confidential Information shall be the sole and exclusive property
     of Discloser ("Assigned IP").

4.2  Recipient hereby assigns to Discloser all right, title, and
     interest in and to any Assigned IP, including all patent,
     copyright, trade secret, and other intellectual property rights.

4.3  Recipient shall promptly disclose any Assigned IP to Discloser
     and shall execute any documents necessary to perfect Discloser's
     ownership rights.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. TERM AND TERMINATION

5.1  This Agreement shall be effective as of the date first written
     above and shall continue for a period of three (3) years, unless
     earlier terminated.

5.2  Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time by providing
     thirty (30) days written notice to the other Party.

5.3  Upon termination, Recipient shall promptly return or destroy all
     Confidential Information in its possession and certify in writing
     that it has done so.

5.4  The obligations set forth in Sections 2, 3, and 4 shall survive
     termination of this Agreement.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. REMEDIES

6.1  Recipient acknowledges that any breach of this Agreement may cause
     irreparable harm to Discloser for which monetary damages would be
     inadequate. Accordingly, Discloser shall be entitled to seek
     injunctive relief in addition to any other remedies available at
     law or in equity.

6.2  In the event of a breach, the prevailing Party in any legal action
     shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorney's fees and
     costs from the non-prevailing Party.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. GENERAL PROVISIONS

7.1  Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed
     in accordance with the laws of the Province of British Columbia
     and the federal laws of Canada applicable therein.

7.2  Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement
     between the Parties regarding the subject matter hereof and
     supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and negotiations.

7.3  Amendment. This Agreement may not be amended except by a written
     instrument signed by both Parties.

7.4  Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is found to be
     invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain
     in full force and effect.
